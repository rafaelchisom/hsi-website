What we do

We fix the parts of healthcare that better coordination can solve.

Most preventable harm in healthcare doesn't happen because of a lack of knowledge or technology — it happens in the gaps between teams, systems, and handovers. Delayed referrals, poor handovers, fragmented records, unclear responsibilities.

We identify those gaps, design practical interventions, and build tools that make better coordination possible — starting with neonatal intensive care networks.

We start with the problem

Before designing anything, we spend time understanding exactly where and why coordination breaks down — through direct conversations with frontline teams.

We build practical tools

Simple, usable software that clinical teams actually adopt — not impressive-looking systems that sit unused. Minimum viable, maximum impact.

We share everything openly

Our findings and tools are published openly so others can learn, adapt, and build on what we create.

Who we are

Health Systems Initiative is an independent research and innovation organisation focused on improving how healthcare is delivered at a systems level. We are based in the United Kingdom, with a focus on challenges relevant both domestically and across low- and middle-income countries.

We combine direct clinical insight with software engineering and health systems research — not to build technology for its own sake, but to solve specific, well-defined problems in how care is coordinated.

Our approach

Before designing anything, we spend time understanding the problem in detail — through conversations with frontline workers, mapping of existing processes, and analysis of where things break down and why.

We prototype early, test with real users, and iterate. We build the minimum viable thing that solves the actual problem. Everything we create is documented so that others can learn from it, adapt it, and build on it.

Where possible, we open-source our tools and publish our findings openly, so that the work can have impact beyond the organisations we work with directly.

What we are building toward

Our long-term aim is to build a body of evidence and a set of reusable tools that can help any clinical network — regardless of geography or resource level — coordinate patient care more effectively.

We're starting with neonatal intensive care because the stakes are high and the coordination failures are well-documented. But the patterns we are working on apply across many areas of healthcare.

NICU Network — Healthcare Referral System

A web-based coordination platform for neonatal intensive care networks — reducing the time between referral decision and patient admission.

Neonatal care Web application 2025

When a premature baby needs transferring between NICUs, the referral and coordination process is often manual, slow, and error-prone. Our platform gives clinical teams across multiple hospitals a shared, real-time view of bed availability, patient status, and transfer logistics.

The goal is simple: reduce the time between the decision to transfer and the patient's arrival at the receiving unit — one of the most critical windows in neonatal emergency care.

  • Real-time bed availability across all network hospitals
  • Structured referral request & acceptance workflow
  • Clinical handover documentation with checklist
  • Transfer tracking and escalation alerts
  • Full audit trail for every referral episode
The demo is fully interactive — explore the complete referral workflow from initial request through to transfer completion.
