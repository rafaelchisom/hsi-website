Who we are

Health Systems Initiative is an independent research and innovation organisation focused on improving how healthcare is delivered at a systems level. We work across sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and the United Kingdom, partnering with hospitals, governments, and NGOs.

Our team brings together clinicians, software engineers, health economists, and policy experts to develop practical solutions that can be implemented at scale — not just in well-resourced settings, but where they are needed most.

Our approach

We believe lasting change in healthcare requires both technical rigour and deep contextual understanding. Every project begins with fieldwork: talking to frontline workers, mapping existing processes, and identifying the precise points of failure.

From there, we prototype rapidly, test iteratively, and document everything so insights can be replicated and scaled by others. We publish open-access reports and, where possible, open-source our software tools.

Our values

Equity first

Every solution must be accessible to those with fewest resources, not only those with the most.

Evidence-based

We build on what data shows, not what is fashionable or easy.

Open by default

We publish our methods, share our tools, and credit all partners.

Long-term thinking

We optimise for systems change over years, not quick wins over months.

NICU Network — Healthcare Referral System

An intelligent referral and patient transfer platform for neonatal intensive care networks, designed to reduce transfer delays and improve clinical handover quality across a hospital network.

Status
● Live demo
Domain
Neonatal / Critical care
Type
Web application UI
Year
2025

This interactive demo showcases a NICU referral coordination platform. Hospitals can view real-time bed capacity across a network, initiate and track patient transfers, assign receiving clinicians, and complete structured clinical handovers — all within a single interface. The system is designed to reduce the time between referral decision and patient admission, a critical window in neonatal emergencies.

Key features
Real-time bed availability dashboard across network hospitals
Structured referral request and acceptance workflow
Clinical handover documentation with checklist
Transfer tracking with estimated arrival time
Escalation alerts for high-acuity patients
Full audit trail for each referral episode
